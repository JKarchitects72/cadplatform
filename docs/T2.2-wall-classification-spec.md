# T2.2 — Wall classification hardening (spec)

Status: PROPOSED. Successor to T2.1 (convention-independent layer separation +
geometry-based classification, shipped in `image_engine/layer_separation.py` and
`cad_pipeline/classify.py`).

This spec is **convention-independent by mandate**: color/linetype/lineweight are
grouping signals only, never meaning. No rule below may introduce a
color-to-meaning mapping. If an implementation finds itself writing `if color == X`
to mean "wall", it has violated this spec.

## Context: what T2.1 established

Stage A (separation) works: on the TEST_1 real plan it discovered 8 layer-groups
from the drawing's own palette, convention-independently, with scale-stable
verdicts (7/8 stable across 1.0/1.2/1.5 mm/px — scale is **not** the bottleneck).

Stage B (classification) is a structural success but under-rejects two phantom
sources. Per-group result at 1.2 mm/px (TEST_1):

| group (reading)        | label       | emits | verdict                         |
|------------------------|-------------|------:|---------------------------------|
| blue furniture/casework| OTHER       | 0     | correctly rejected              |
| gray grid/dims/hatch   | STAIRS      | 0     | rejected (right outcome)        |
| magenta wall poché     | WALL        | 8     | real walls recovered            |
| lt-blue outlines       | WALL        | 6     | mostly real                     |
| green outlines         | WALL        | 2     | real                            |
| black linework         | WALL_REVIEW | 1     | visible near-miss               |
| red staircases         | WALL        | 17    | **FALSE POSITIVE (leak)**       |
| yellow hatch           | WALL        | 7     | **FALSE POSITIVE (leak)**       |

Baseline: T2.0 emitted 58 walls (~30 phantom, furniture-dominated). T2.1 emits 41
(~24 phantom). Raw phantom count is only modestly lower, but furniture (the #1 T2.0
source) is fully rejected and the remaining phantoms are now **isolated into two
named groups** — addressable, not smeared. T2.2 turns that isolation into rejection.

## Goal

Reduce TEST_1 emitted phantoms from ~24 toward ~0 **without** losing the real walls
recovered in T2.1, and **without** any convention-specific tuning. Net target:
precision and recall both materially up vs T2.1, verdicts still scale-stable.

---

## Finding 1 — Spatial-local stairs detection

**Problem.** `classify._stair_runs` pools all co-oriented segments across the whole
sheet before looking for a constant-pitch run. Two separate staircases' treads
interleave in that global ordering, breaking the constant pitch, so the run test
never fires — the red-staircase group falls through the cascade to WALL (17 phantom
walls).

**Requirement.** Detect tread ladders **locally**: cluster co-oriented segments
into spatial bands (by perpendicular position AND shared along-axis extent) before
testing for a run of ≥N equally-spaced parallels. A region with such a run is
STAIRS regardless of how many other ladders exist elsewhere on the sheet.

**Acceptance.** The red-staircase group classifies as STAIRS (emits 0). A
synthetic two-staircase fixture (two tread ladders at different pitches) is
correctly rejected; a genuine wall run of evenly spaced parallel partitions is
**not** misread as stairs (treads have no paired second face at wall thickness —
use that to disambiguate).

## Finding 2 — Hatch as a fill REGION, not lines

**Problem.** Yellow poché hatch is a dense field of short parallel diagonal
strokes. Its boundary/edges pair at wall spacing, so the group classifies as WALL
(7 phantom walls). The existing `HATCH` test (`short_frac>0.6 & diag_frac>0.5 &
in_band<2`) is too strict to fire here.

**Requirement.** Recognize hatch as a **region**: a bounded area of high-density,
consistent-angle, regularly-spaced short parallel segments. A group dominated by
such a region is HATCH and emits no wall lines. Crucially, a detected hatch region
is **positive evidence** for a wall when it lies between two long parallel faces
(possibly in another group — see Finding 3), not a wall on its own.

**Acceptance.** The yellow-hatch group classifies as HATCH (emits 0). Hatch density
and angle-consistency thresholds are expressed in scale-independent / structural
terms (fraction of group, angular spread), never per-drawing constants.

## Finding 3 — Cross-group wall assembly (the architectural fix)

**Problem (biggest).** A poché wall's two **faces** and its **hatch fill** are
frequently drawn on different layers and therefore land in different discovered
color-groups (TEST_1: faces magenta, hatch yellow/gray). Per-group classification
structurally cannot see the whole wall — a wall's evidence is split across groups.
This both costs recall (faces alone may not reach the WALL threshold) and causes
leaks (hatch alone pairs and looks wall-ish).

**Requirement.** Introduce a wall-assembly step that reasons **across** groups:
- collect candidate long parallel faces from all groups,
- pair faces at standardized thickness even when the two faces come from different
  groups,
- treat a hatch region (Finding 2) sitting between a face pair as confirmation,
- emit the assembled wall once; suppress the hatch lines and the bare faces that
  were consumed.

This stays convention-independent: assembly keys on geometry (parallel, spacing,
enclosure, hatch-between), never on which color a face came from. Group identity
becomes a hint for de-duplication, not a meaning.

**Architecture note.** Assembly is pure geometry → lives in `cad_pipeline`. Stage A
must therefore hand cad_pipeline the per-group segments together (it already does);
cad_pipeline gains an assembly pass upstream of per-group labeling, or replacing
it. One-way data flow (Rule 1) is preserved.

**Acceptance.** A synthetic wall whose faces and hatch are split across three groups
is assembled into one wall. On TEST_1, real poché walls are emitted once (no double
-counting between the face group and the hatch group), and recall does not regress.

## Finding 4 — Split heterogeneous catch-all groups

**Problem.** The dominant gray group (~49% of ink) mixes walls?/grid/dimensions/
hatch. It is labeled by whatever cascade branch fires first (STAIRS) and the
heterogeneity detector under-reported it (only the stairs evidence fired). Color-
primary v1 cannot separate same-color sub-populations.

**Requirement (v2 of Stage A metadata).** When a single color-group shows distinct
structural sub-populations — bimodal stroke width, or a mix of (sheet-spanning
unpaired lines) + (short clustered) + (paired faces) — **split it** by the secondary
grouping signals already carried as metadata (lineweight, dash/linetype) before
Stage B labels it. At minimum, the heterogeneity detector must *flag* every such
group (today it misses the catch-all). Splitting is the fix; flagging is the floor.

**Acceptance.** The gray catch-all is either split into sub-groups (grid vs dims vs
any wall linework) or, at minimum, reported as heterogeneous with the detected
sub-populations named. No convention-specific logic.

## Cross-cutting acceptance criteria

- SYNTH regression stays byte-for-byte (single group → WALL → 6 walls).
- TEST_1 (gitignored, local only): emitted phantoms approach 0 while the T2.1 real
  walls remain; verdicts stay scale-stable across 1.0/1.2/1.5 mm/px.
- No `if color == X` anywhere. New tests assert classification on STRUCTURE only.
- CLAUDE.md rules 1–7 hold (one-way flow, vision = semantics/scale only, ByLayer,
  native entities, mandatory `dxf.audit()` validation).
- Rejected-but-plausible walls still land visibly on A-WALL-REVIEW (non-plotting),
  never silently dropped.

## Deferred (not T2.2)

Vision-based scale (still M5; T2.1 showed scale is not the current bottleneck).
Single-line (non-poché) wall handling beyond the existing review routing. Curve
detection, door/window BLOCKs.
